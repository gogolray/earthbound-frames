Earthbound Frames

A Visual Archive by Ray Kawshik
“An earthbound in a closed cosmos.”

A photographic archive of atmospheres, ruins, thresholds, weather, cities, and nonhuman presences. These are not simply images of places, but encounters with distributed worlds and fragile planetary coexistence.

Curatorial Note

Earthbound Frames emerges from a practice of attention rather than spectacle. It explores ordinary spaces where the world quietly exceeds the human: rain-soaked stations, abandoned walls, railway thresholds, roadside trees, and cities held together by weather. These are not landscapes as scenery. They are negotiations. The frame is not a border, but a temporary settlement between seeing and being seen.
